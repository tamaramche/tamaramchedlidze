Algorithms for Drawing Graphs with Constraints

27 February 2025

Drawing constraints are restrictions on a graph visualization which may stem from an interaction process or an application semantics. When interacting with a graph visualization environment, the user may want to restrict positions of the vertices and the shape of the edges, but still use a specific drawing algorithm. For instance, the vertices which represent important entities in the graph may have to be placed in the middle, or on the top of the drawing; the edges that represent crucial connections may have to be drawn with simpler curves than other edges. Without integrating drawing constraints into visualization algorithms, we leave it to the user to reposition the vertices and the edges the way she likes after running the algorithm,- a process that can be very time consuming even for a small number of vertices and edges, as this requires touching a large part of the visualization to create space or to route the edges appropriately. Thus, drawing constraints have become a common part of network visualization algorithms. #anchors-in-markdown

On a high level, drawing constraints can be characterized as vertex and edge constraints, depending on whether they impose restrictions on vertices or edges. In my work, I have studied theoretical foundations of vertex constraints provided by: their exact coordinates their exact coordinates, by a line or a set of them and by polygonal regions.
